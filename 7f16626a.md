## Question 1: Short Answer Questions (15 marks) - 3 marks each

### (a) Spring Constant Relationship with Mass

**Question:** Two masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ on smooth plane, connected by spring. When $m_1$ held fixed, $m_2$ oscillates with period $T_{12}$. Show: $m_2 T_{21}^2 = m_1 T_{12}^2$

**Solution:**

When $m_1$ is fixed and $m_2$ oscillates on spring constant $k$:
$$T_{12} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m_2}{k}}$$

When $m_2$ is fixed and $m_1$ oscillates:
$$T_{21} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m_1}{k}}$$

**Taking squares:**
$$T_{12}^2 = 4\pi^2\frac{m_2}{k}, \quad T_{21}^2 = 4\pi^2\frac{m_1}{k}$$

**Multiply:**
$$m_1 T_{12}^2 = m_1 \cdot 4\pi^2\frac{m_2}{k} = 4\pi^2\frac{m_1 m_2}{k}$$

$$m_2 T_{21}^2 = m_2 \cdot 4\pi^2\frac{m_1}{k} = 4\pi^2\frac{m_1 m_2}{k}$$

Therefore: $\boxed{m_1 T_{12}^2 = m_2 T_{21}^2}$ ✓

### (b) Compound Pendulum and Ring Oscillations

**Compound Pendulum:** Rigid body pivoted at fixed point, oscillating under gravity.

**For circular ring** of diameter $d$, hanging on nail:

Moment of inertia about nail: $I = I_{cm} + Md^2/4 = MR^2 + MR^2 = 2MR^2$

Torque: $\tau = -Mgd/2 \sin\theta \approx -Mgd/2 \cdot \theta$ (for small $\theta$)

$$I\alpha = -Mgd/2 \cdot \theta$$

$$2MR^2 \cdot \frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} = -Mgd/2 \cdot \theta$$

$$\frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} + \frac{gd}{4 \times 2R^2}\theta = 0$$

Since $d = 2R$:
$$\frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} + \frac{g}{4R}\theta = 0$$

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{g}{4R}}$$

**Time period:**
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{4R}{g}} = \boxed{4\pi\sqrt{\frac{R}{g}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{d}{g}}}$$

---

### (c) Energy Exchange in Coupled Pendulum

**Mechanism:**

1. **Normal Modes:** System has two normal frequencies
2. **Initial oscillation:** If one pendulum oscillates, it transfers energy through coupling
3. **Beat phenomenon:** Energy oscillates between pendulums
4. **Period of energy transfer:** Related to beat frequency $\omega_1 - \omega_2$
5. **Maximum transfer:** When $\omega_1 \approx \omega_2$ (weak coupling)

**Energy oscillates between coupled oscillators at frequency** $|\omega_1 - \omega_2|$

### (d) LCR Circuit - Oscillatory Discharge

**Question:** L = 0.5 H, C = 5 µF. Find max R for oscillatory discharge.

**Solution:**

For oscillatory (underdamped) response:
$$R < R_{critical} = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{L}{C}}$$

$$R_c = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{0.5}{5 \times 10^{-6}}} = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{100,000}$$

$$= \frac{1}{2} \times 316.2 = \boxed{158.1 \text{ Ω}}$$

Maximum R for oscillatory: $\boxed{R_{max} \approx 158 \text{ Ω}}$

### (e) Particle on String - Transverse Oscillations

**Question:** String length 3a, tension T, particle mass m at distance a from end. Find period.

**Solution:**

Particle is on two string segments:
- Segment 1: length $a$, effective mass involved
- Segment 2: length $2a$

**Effective system:** Two coupled segments

For small transverse displacement $y$:

Restoring force: $F = -\frac{2Ty}{a}$ (from geometry)

Mass: $m$

$$m\frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -\frac{2Ty}{a}$$

$$\omega^2 = \frac{2T}{ma}$$

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{2T}{ma}}$$

**Time period:**
$$\boxed{T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{ma}{2T}} = \pi\sqrt{\frac{2ma}{T}}}$$

## Question 2: Spring Systems (15 marks)

### (a) Spring with Finite Mass (10 marks)

**Question:** Spring constant k, finite mass $m_s$, loaded with mass M. Show: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{M + m_s/3}{k}}$

**Solution:**

For spring with distributed mass, effective mass is not simply $m_s$. 

Consider spring element at distance $x$ from top, with length element $dx$:

Velocity varies linearly: $v(x) = v_0 \frac{x}{L}$

Kinetic energy of spring:
$$KE_{spring} = \frac{1}{2}\int_0^L \frac{m_s}{L}v^2(x) dx = \frac{1}{2}\frac{m_s}{L} \cdot v_0^2 \int_0^L \frac{x^2}{L^2} dx$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}\frac{m_s}{L} \cdot v_0^2 \cdot \frac{L}{3} = \frac{1}{6}m_s v_0^2$$

Total KE:
$$KE_{total} = \frac{1}{2}Mv_0^2 + \frac{1}{6}m_s v_0^2 = \frac{1}{2}(M + m_s/3)v_0^2$$

Equivalent mass: $M_{eff} = M + m_s/3$

**Period:**
$$\boxed{T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{M + m_s/3}{k}}}$$

### (b) Two Springs Connected (5 marks)

**Series connection (Fig 2a):** Spring constants $k_1$ and $k_2$

Equivalent spring constant:
$$\frac{1}{k_{eq}} = \frac{1}{k_1} + \frac{1}{k_2}$$

$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k_{eq}}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m(1/k_1 + 1/k_2)}{1}} = \boxed{2\pi\sqrt{\frac{mk_1 + mk_2}{k_1 k_2}}}$$

**Parallel connection (Fig 2b):** Springs attached to same mass

Equivalent spring constant:
$$k_{eq} = k_1 + k_2$$

$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k_1 + k_2}}$$

## Question 3: Superposition and Beats (15 marks)

### (a) Beats Formation (5 marks)

**Question:** Two SHM of frequency 8 Hz and 6 Hz, amplitude 1.0 cm, in phase.

**Solution:**

$$x_1 = A\cos(2\pi \cdot 8 \cdot t) = A\cos(16\pi t)$$
$$x_2 = A\cos(2\pi \cdot 6 \cdot t) = A\cos(12\pi t)$$

**Resultant displacement:**
$$x = x_1 + x_2 = A[\cos(16\pi t) + \cos(12\pi t)]$$

Using $\cos A + \cos B = 2\cos(\frac{A+B}{2})\cos(\frac{A-B}{2})$:

$$x = 2A\cos(\pi t)\cos(14\pi t)$$

This is oscillation at 14 Hz (average frequency) with amplitude modulated by $\cos(\pi t)$.

**Beat frequency:**
$$f_{beat} = |f_1 - f_2| = |8 - 6| = \boxed{2 \text{ Hz}}$$

**Graphs:**
- Individual sinusoids at 8 and 6 Hz
- Resultant: 14 Hz carrier modulated by 2 Hz envelope

### (b) Lissajous Figure (6,4) marks

**Question:** Two perpendicular waves, frequency ratio 1:2, phase difference φ.

$$x = A\sin(\omega t)$$
$$y = B\sin(2\omega t + \varphi)$$

**Analytical solution:**

From first equation: $\sin(\omega t) = x/A$

$$y = B\sin(2\omega t + \varphi) = B[\sin(2\omega t)\cos\varphi + \cos(2\omega t)\sin\varphi]$$

Using $\sin(2\omega t) = 2\sin(\omega t)\cos(\omega t)$ and $\cos(2\omega t) = 1 - 2\sin^2(\omega t)$:

$$y = B[2(x/A)\sqrt{1-(x/A)^2}\cos\varphi + (1-2(x/A)^2)\sin\varphi]$$

**For φ = π/4:**

The figure is a **figure-eight** pattern with two loops, slightly asymmetric due to phase difference.

## Question 4: Damped Harmonic Oscillator (15 marks)

### (a) Damped Oscillator Equation (10 marks)

**Equation of motion:**
$$m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + 2m\gamma\frac{dx}{dt} + m\omega_0^2 x = 0$$

where $\gamma$ = damping constant, $\omega_0$ = natural frequency

**For underdamped case** ($\gamma < \omega_0$):

Characteristic equation: $r^2 + 2\gamma r + \omega_0^2 = 0$

$$r = -\gamma \pm i\sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \gamma^2} = -\gamma \pm i\omega'$$

where $\omega' = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \gamma^2}$ (damped frequency)

**Solution:**
$$x(t) = e^{-\gamma t}(A\cos(\omega' t) + B\sin(\omega' t))$$

Or: $x(t) = A_0 e^{-\gamma t}\cos(\omega' t + \phi)$

**Amplitude decays exponentially:** $A(t) = A_0 e^{-\gamma t}$

**Oscillations occur at damped frequency:** $\omega' = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \gamma^2}$

---

### (b) Damping Calculations (5 marks)

**Question:** Amplitude reduced to 1/10 after 100 oscillations. Period = 2 s. Find damping constant and relaxation time.

**Solution:**

After n oscillations: $x_n = x_0 e^{-\gamma nT} = x_0/10$

$$e^{-\gamma \cdot 100 \cdot 2} = 1/10$$

$$e^{-200\gamma} = 0.1$$

$$-200\gamma = \ln(0.1) = -2.303$$

$$\gamma = \frac{2.303}{200} = \boxed{0.01151 \text{ s}^{-1}}$$

**Relaxation time:** $\tau = 1/\gamma = \boxed{86.9 \text{ s}}$

## Question 5: Coupled Oscillators (15 marks)

### (a) Normal Mode Frequencies (10 marks)

**Two masses m connected by three springs k.**

Using Lagrangian method or Newton's laws, the coupled equations give:

**Normal frequencies:**
$$\omega_1^2 = \frac{k}{m}, \quad \omega_2^2 = \frac{3k}{m}$$

**Amplitude ratios:**
- **Slower mode** ($\omega_1$): $\frac{A_2}{A_1} = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}$
- **Faster mode** ($\omega_2$): $\frac{A_2}{A_1} = \frac{\sqrt{5}+1}{2}$

These are the golden ratio and its complement!

### (b) Standing Wave (5 marks)

**Question:** Show $f(x,t) = A\sin(kx)\cos(kvt)$ satisfies wave equation.

**Solution:**

Wave equation: $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial t^2} = v^2\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}$

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} = -Av\sin(kx)\sin(kvt)$$

$$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial t^2} = -Ak^2v^2\sin(kx)\cos(kvt)$$

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = Ak\cos(kx)\cos(kvt)$$

$$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = -Ak^2\sin(kx)\cos(kvt)$$

$$v^2\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = -Av^2k^2\sin(kx)\cos(kvt)$$

**Left side = Right side ✓**

**Express as sum of traveling waves:**

Using $\sin A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\sin(A+B) + \sin(A-B)]$:

$$f = \frac{A}{2}[\sin(kx+kvt) + \sin(kx-kvt)]$$

This represents:
- Wave traveling right: $\sin(kx - kvt)$
- Wave traveling left: $\sin(kx + kvt)$

Standing wave = superposition of equal amplitude counter-propagating waves.
