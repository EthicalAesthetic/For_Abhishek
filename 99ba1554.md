## Question 1: Short Answer Questions (18 marks) - 3 marks each

### (i) Block on Frictionless Platform

**Question:** Block mass 2 kg on frictionless platform. Coefficient static friction = 0.6. Find max acceleration so block doesn't slip.

**Solution:**

For the block not to slip, friction force must provide centripetal acceleration:
$$f_s \leq \mu_s N = \mu_s mg$$

Maximum friction force:
$$f_{max} = 0.6 \times 2 \times 10 = 12 \text{ N}$$

This provides maximum acceleration:
$$a_{max} = \frac{f_{max}}{m} = \frac{12}{2} = \boxed{6 \text{ m/s}^2}$$

### (ii) Work Done in Force Field

**Question:** Find work from A(-2,1,3) to B(1,-2,-1) in field 
$$\vec{F} = (y^2z - 6xz^2)\hat{i} + 2xyz^2\hat{j} + (3xy^2z^2 - 6x^2z)\hat{k}$$

**Solution:**

Check if conservative: $\nabla \times \vec{F} = 0$?

After calculation, this field is conservative.

Scalar potential: $U(x,y,z) = xy^2z - 3x^2z^2$

Work done:
$$W = U(1,-2,-1) - U(-2,1,3)$$

$$= [1 \times 4 \times (-1) - 3 \times 1 \times 1] - [(-2) \times 1 \times 3 - 3 \times 4 \times 9]$$

$$= [-4 - 3] - [-6 - 108] = -7 - (-114) = \boxed{107 \text{ J}}$$

### (iii) Cosmic Ray Proton - Time Dilation

**Question:** Proton energy = 10²⁰ eV crosses galaxy diameter 10⁵ light years. Time in rest frame?

**Solution:**

Rest energy: $E_0 = m_pc^2 = 938$ MeV = $9.38 \times 10^8$ eV

Lorentz factor:
$$\gamma = \frac{E}{E_0} = \frac{10^{20}}{9.38 \times 10^8} = 1.066 \times 10^{11}$$

Velocity: $v \approx c$ (since $\gamma >> 1$)

Distance in lab frame: $d = 10^5$ light-years

**Proper time (rest frame):**

Time in lab frame: $t_{lab} = \frac{d}{c} = 10^5$ years

Time in proton frame:
$$t_{proper} = \frac{t_{lab}}{\gamma} = \frac{10^5}{1.066 \times 10^{11}} = \boxed{9.38 \times 10^{-7} \text{ years}}$$

Or: $\approx 30$ seconds

### (iv) Center of Mass - Four Particles

**Question:** Three particles: 10g at origin, 20g at (1,-2,3), 30g at (?, ?, ?). Fourth particle 40g at (?, ?, ?). CM of three at (1,-2,3). Find fourth position for combined CM at (1,1,1).

**Solution:**

Let fourth particle be at $(x, y, z)$.

For three particles, total: 60g with CM at (1,-2,3)

For four particles with 40g added:
$$\vec{r}_{cm} = \frac{60(1,-2,3) + 40(x,y,z)}{100} = (1,1,1)$$

$$60(1,-2,3) + 40(x,y,z) = 100(1,1,1)$$

$$(60,-120,180) + 40(x,y,z) = (100,100,100)$$

$$40(x,y,z) = (40,220,-80)$$

$$(x,y,z) = \boxed{(1, 5.5, -2)}$$

### (v) Particle in Central Force Field

**Question:** Particle mass $m$ in field $F = -\frac{k}{r^2}$. Total energy $E$. Find speed.

**Solution:**

Energy conservation:
$$E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{L^2}{2mr^2} + U(r)$$

where $U(r) = -\frac{k}{r}$ for inverse-square force.

$$E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{k}{r} + \frac{L^2}{2mr^2}$$

But at given point (without specifying position):

$$\boxed{v = \sqrt{\frac{2}{m}(E + \frac{k}{r} - \frac{L^2}{2mr^2})}}$$

Or more generally: $v = \sqrt{\frac{2E}{m}}$ if we measure from CM at infinity.

### (vi) Angular Momentum

**Question:** Mass 10 kg moving in circle radius 4 m, speed 5 m/s. Find angular momentum about point 3 m from center on perpendicular axis.

**Solution:**

Using $\vec{L} = \vec{r} \times m\vec{v}$

Distance from point to particle: $\sqrt{4^2 + 3^2} = 5$ m

Perpendicular component of velocity relevant for L about the point.

Using: $L = mvr_{\perp}$

where $r_{\perp}$ is perpendicular distance from point to velocity vector.

At different points along the circle, this varies.

Using general formula:
$$L = mvr = 10 \times 5 \times 4 = \boxed{200 \text{ kg·m}^2\text{/s}}$$

(about center of circle)

For point 3m away, calculation is more complex and would need specific position.

## Question 2: Rocket Propulsion (18 marks)

### (i) Principle and Equation of Motion (6 marks)

**Rocket Propulsion:**

By Newton's third law, when rocket expels hot gases at high velocity, reaction force accelerates rocket.

**Equation of Motion:**

At time $t$, rocket has mass $m$ and velocity $v$.

Mass $dm$ ejected with velocity $v_e$ (relative to rocket) in time $dt$.

**Momentum conservation:**
$$m v = (m - dm)(v + dv) + dm(v - u)$$

where $u$ is exhaust velocity relative to ground.

For $v_e$ as relative velocity to rocket:
$$u = v - v_e$$

Simplifying:
$$m dv = v_e dm$$

$$dv = v_e \frac{dm}{m}$$

**Integrating:**
$$V = v_e \ln\left(\frac{M_0}{M}\right)$$

where $M_0$ is initial mass, $M$ is final mass.

For rocket taking off vertically under gravity:
$$m\frac{dv}{dt} = v_e\frac{dm}{dt} - mg$$

where $\frac{dm}{dt}$ is mass burn rate.

### (ii) Rocket Problem (6 marks)

**Question:** Mass burn rate = 0.05$M_0$ kg/s, exhaust velocity = 5 km/s = 5000 m/s. Find velocity and height after 10 s.

**Solution:**

**Velocity:**

$$v = v_e \ln\left(\frac{M_0}{M}\right) - gt$$

At $t = 10$ s:
- Remaining mass: $M = M_0(1 - 0.05 \times 10) = 0.5M_0$
- Velocity from rocket equation: $v = 5000 \ln(2) - 10 \times 10 = 3466 - 100 = 3366$ m/s

**Height:**

Integrating velocity (with variable mass):

Using energy method or numerical integration:

Average velocity $\approx$ 1683 m/s (rough estimate)

Height $\approx 1683 \times 10 = 16,830$ m $\approx \boxed{16.8 \text{ km}}$

(More precise integration needed for exact answer)

### (iii) Center of Mass of Circular Plate with Hole (6 marks)

**Question:** Circular plate diameter 2R, thickness t. Hole diameter R drilled at boundary. Find CM of remaining part.

**Solution:**

Original plate (uniform, density $\rho$):
- Mass: $M_0 = \rho \pi R^2 t$
- CM at origin: $(0, 0)$

Hole (removed disk, diameter R):
- Mass: $M_h = \rho \pi (R/2)^2 t = \frac{\pi \rho t R^2}{4}$
- CM at distance $R$ from center (at boundary)
- Position: $(R, 0)$

**Remaining plate:**
$$M_{rem} = M_0 - M_h = \rho \pi t R^2 - \frac{\pi \rho t R^2}{4} = \frac{3\pi \rho t R^2}{4}$$

**CM position:**
$$x_{cm} = \frac{M_0 \times 0 - M_h \times R}{M_{rem}} = \frac{-\frac{\pi \rho t R^2}{4} \times R}{\frac{3\pi \rho t R^2}{4}}$$

$$= -\frac{R}{3}$$

**Answer:** $\boxed{\text{CM shifts to } (-R/3, 0)}$ 

The CM **moves toward the hole** (opposite direction from hole).

## Question 3: Work-Energy and Lennard-Jones Potential (18 marks)

### (i) Work-Energy Theorem (6 marks)

**Statement:** The net work done by all forces on a body equals the change in kinetic energy.

$$W_{net} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}m v_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}m v_i^2$$

**Derivation:**

$$W = \int \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \int m\vec{a} \cdot d\vec{r}$$

Using $\vec{a} = \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt}$:

$$W = \int m \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} \cdot d\vec{r} = \int m \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{v}$$

$$= m \int \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{v} = \frac{m}{2}\int d(v^2) = \frac{1}{2}m(v_f^2 - v_i^2)$$

Therefore: $W = \Delta KE$ ✓

### (ii) Lennard-Jones Potential (6 marks)

**Potential:**
$$U(r) = 4\epsilon\left[\left(\frac{\sigma}{r}\right)^{12} - \left(\frac{\sigma}{r}\right)^6\right]$$

**Central Force Check:**

Force: $\vec{F} = -\frac{dU}{dr}\hat{r}$ (radial direction only)

This is indeed a **central force** (attractive at larger distances, repulsive at very small distances).

**Equilibrium position:**

$$\frac{dU}{dr} = 4\epsilon\left[-12\frac{\sigma^{12}}{r^{13}} + 6\frac{\sigma^6}{r^7}\right] = 0$$

$$12\frac{\sigma^{12}}{r^{13}} = 6\frac{\sigma^6}{r^7}$$

$$r_{eq} = 2^{1/6}\sigma = \boxed{1.122\sigma}$$

(This is $r_m$ - the minimum position)

**Potential depth:**

$$U(r_{eq}) = 4\epsilon\left[\frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{2}\right] = 4\epsilon\left[-\frac{1}{4}\right] = \boxed{-\epsilon}$$

(So depth is $\epsilon$)

**Frequency of small oscillations:**

Expand about equilibrium: $r = r_m + x$, $x << r_m$

$$U(r_m + x) \approx U(r_m) + \frac{1}{2}\frac{d^2U}{dr^2}\bigg|_{r_m} x^2$$

Computing second derivative and evaluating:

$$k_{eff} = \frac{d^2U}{dr^2}\bigg|_{r_m} = 72\epsilon/\sigma^2$$

For two identical atoms: $\mu = m/2$

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k_{eff}}{\mu}} = \sqrt{\frac{144\epsilon}{m\sigma^2}} = \boxed{\frac{12}{sigma}\sqrt{\frac{\epsilon}{m}}}$$

### (iii) Plank-Ball Collision (6 marks)

**Question:** Plank length $2l$, mass $M$ on frictionless table. Ball mass $m$, speed $V_0$ strikes end. Find final velocity if mechanical energy conserved and final motion is along original direction.

**Solution:**

**Conservation of momentum:**
$$mV_0 = (m + M)V_f$$

where $V_f$ is final common velocity.

$$V_f = \frac{mV_0}{m + M}$$

**Mechanical energy conservation:**

Initial KE: $\frac{1}{2}mV_0^2$

Final KE: $\frac{1}{2}(m + M)V_f^2 + \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$

For plank rotating about CM: $I = \frac{M(2l)^2}{12} = \frac{Ml^2}{3}$

This involves collision dynamics. For inelastic collision with no rotation:

$$\boxed{V_f = \frac{mV_0}{m + M}}$$

If rotation is significant, velocity is reduced further.
